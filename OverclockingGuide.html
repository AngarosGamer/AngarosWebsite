Overclocking Guide

Made by Plerion#6666 heavily based on Happy Pill#9683's guide - Last update 6/15/2022 @9:46 UTC-8

Overclocking allows you to:

  • Get more earnings
  • Lower power usage
  • Increase the life of your GPU

  • Internet Connection
  • A GPU with 3GB+ VRAM
  • 500MB disk space
  • Have installed MSI Afterburner, found here.

When tuning a GPU for mining on Ethash/Etchash, the first step is to start pushing the memory to its absolutely limit before it crashes or becomes unstable. Basically, just start moving the memory clock slider in MSI afterburner up by around 50mhz at a time until you error out (sometimes youâ€™ll get worse hashrate without noticeable errors, so watch out for that), start artifacting, or your display driver dies (a simple restart will fix the latter, and the former can be remedied by backing off the overclock a little). Make sure to be doing all of this WHILE mining!

When tuning a GPU for most other algorithms you want to follow a simmilar process but apply it to the core aswell, just in smaller increments.

  • MEMORY ONLY
  • For some time savings, you can usually use the following as starting points (use the process above, just start from these figures rather than 0) for your applicable cards:
  • For GDDR5 Turing cards (most notably the 1650, 1660): +500
  • For GDDR6 Turing cards (other GTX 16 and RTX 20 cards): +700-800
  • Turing Max Q cards: +500
  • Ampere (RTX 30) cards: +1000*

Start lowering the power limit until you start losing hashrate. You want it to be as low as possible without sacrificing performance.

Keep in mind that you choose what sacrifices you want to make, for example if you get 0.1MH more but it costs 10 watts, you may want to keep the power lower, of cours it's your choice.

Now that your card is pushing itself harder, you may need to increase your fan speed to keep it in the safe zone (ideally you want your card to never hit above 80 Celsius)

This is also a chance to lower your fan speed if the card is well under 80C and you think it's too loud

To make everything easier save these settings to a profile but do NOT enable apply on Windows startup doing so could cause an infinite crash loop when you boot up that can only be escaped by holding the Ctrl key right after login

If you plan to game, press the reset button to reset your oc or select your gaming oc if you have one to prevent accidental crashes
